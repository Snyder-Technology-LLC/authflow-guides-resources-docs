Pricing your GPT correctly is crucial for success. Price too high and you'll lose potential customers; too low and you'll leave money on the table. This guide helps you find the sweet spot.

## Factors to Consider

Before setting your price, consider:

### 1. Value Delivered
- What problem does your GPT solve?
- How much time/money does it save users?
- What's the alternative cost? (hiring a consultant, using multiple tools, etc.)

### 2. Target Audience
- Who are your ideal customers?
- What's their budget capacity?
- Are they individuals or businesses?

### 3. Competition
- What do similar GPTs charge?
- What features do you offer that others don't?
- Can you differentiate on quality or specialization?

### 4. Your Costs
- Consider Authflow fees (10% per transaction)
- Consider Stripe fees (~2.9% + $0.30)
- Factor in your time investment

## Common Pricing Tiers

### Low Entry Point ($1-5/month)

**Best for:**
- Simple utility tools
- High-volume, mass-market products
- Building a large subscriber base quickly

**Pros:**
- Low barrier to entry
- Attracts more users
- Good for viral growth

**Cons:**
- Need high volume for meaningful revenue
- May attract less committed users

### Mid-Range ($5-20/month)

**Best for:**
- Most GPTs and general tools
- Professional productivity tools
- Content creation assistants

**Pros:**
- Balance of accessibility and value
- Sustainable revenue per user
- Most common and accepted range

**Cons:**
- Competitive space
- Need to demonstrate clear value

### Premium ($20-100/month)

**Best for:**
- Specialized professional tools
- Business-focused GPTs
- High-value niches (legal, medical, finance)

**Pros:**
- Fewer but more committed users
- Higher perceived value
- Better revenue per customer

**Cons:**
- Smaller potential audience
- Higher expectations from users

### Enterprise ($100+/month)

**Best for:**
- Team/business solutions
- Custom integrations
- Specialized industry tools

**Pros:**
- Significant revenue per customer
- Long-term relationships
- Can offer dedicated support

**Cons:**
- Longer sales cycles
- Higher support requirements

## Pricing Psychology Tips

### Use Charm Pricing
- $9.99 feels significantly less than $10.00
- $19/month feels more accessible than $20/month

### Anchor with Annual Pricing
- Show annual price as the "best value"
- Offer 15-20% discount for annual
- Monthly becomes the "convenient" option

### Highlight Value, Not Features
- "Save 10 hours per week" beats "10 features included"
- Focus on outcomes, not inputs

### Offer Multiple Tiers (if applicable)
- Make the middle tier the most attractive
- Use the high tier to make the middle look reasonable

## Testing and Iteration

### Start Conservative
- It's easier to lower prices than raise them
- You can always add promotional discounts

### Use Analytics
- Track conversion rates at different price points
- Monitor churn rates
- A/B test pricing when possible

### Listen to Feedback
- If many users say it's too expensive, consider adjusting
- If no one complains, you might be underpriced

## Example Pricing Calculation

**Scenario:** Professional Writing Assistant GPT

| Factor | Analysis |
|--------|----------|
| Value | Saves 5+ hours/week for writers |
| Audience | Freelance writers, content marketers |
| Competition | Similar tools charge $15-30/month |
| Differentiation | Specialized for long-form content |

**Suggested Pricing:**
- Monthly: $19/month
- Annual: $149/year (save $79 = 35% discount)
- Free trial: 7 days

**Revenue projection (100 users):**
- Monthly revenue: $1,900
- Less Authflow (10%): -$190
- Less Stripe (~3%): -$57
- Net monthly: ~$1,653
